# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Primary users are Grand Summit 15th administrators who manage BCC and MCC registrations, monitor team readiness, validate payment and task uploads, and export operational data to Google Sheets. Participants use the public event pages, profile flow, team registration pages, and upload workflows.

## Product Purpose

This product runs the Grand Summit 15th website and competition registration system. It should help participants register without admin back-and-forth, and help admins see registration health, payment status, submission readiness, and export state quickly enough to make event operations decisions.

## Brand Personality

Focused, credible, energetic. The public surface should feel event-led and confident; the authenticated admin surface should prioritize clarity, density, and operational trust over decoration.

## Anti-references

Avoid generic SaaS dashboards, overly decorative glass panels, oversized marketing-style admin heroes, low-contrast muted text, and visual treatments that make operational data harder to scan. Avoid catch-all middleware or UI choices that hide data needed by admins during submission windows.

## Design Principles

1. Surface operational state first: admins should see counts, blockers, and late or missing submissions before opening individual rows.
2. Keep public brand and admin utility distinct: preserve Grand Summit colors, but make admin screens denser and calmer than campaign pages.
3. Mirror participant state in admin reporting: any participant-side registration or submission state should be visible in admin and export surfaces.
4. Make exceptional cases obvious: late submissions, incomplete preliminary files, unpaid teams, and closed registrations need clear labels.
5. Prefer familiar product UI patterns: tables, filters, status pills, and progressive row details beat custom interaction patterns.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Aim for WCAG AA contrast on admin text, controls, and status labels. Keep controls keyboard reachable, avoid motion that blocks content visibility, and maintain reduced-motion friendly animations through Framer Motion patterns that do not gate content.
